Random points inside a circle of radius r around 0,0: the problem is easily solved thinking in radial coordinates: we want a point with a random distance from the center, "r", and a random angle "t". Since the surface of a circle goes with the square power of the radius, and we want uniform coverage of such surface (i.e. uniformly distributed points) the random radius values have to be sampled with square root weight.
